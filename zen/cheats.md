# Cheat Codes

## 0. Don't take life seriously.
Life experience is an illusion dictated by computation.

Even if you inject "meaning" into certain aspects of the illusion, they will die anyway. It will die anyway. You will die anyway. Memento Mori.

When you take nothing seriously, you enjoy everything automatically.

&nbsp;

If you truly embody this law, you can stop here, sit back & relax, and enjoy the movie.

If you want to get something out of this illusion, here are the cheat codes for the game.

---

## 1. You lack emotional strength.
You've been fed countless quotes from countless sources countless times. Your brain already knows what to do to maximize your long-term utility for survival on a rational level. In fact, you've probably heard of the cheat codes below already.

But when it comes to execution, you forget them all.

Why? Emotions. They consistently steer you away from awareness and clarity.

As soon as emotions get to you, it's over. You can memorize all the cheat codes below by heart, and it won't matter. The more you are controlled by emotions, the more you are on autopilot, the less likely you can get anything out of this game.

On the other hand, if you've mastered emotional strength, you've already won the game. Emotions arise. You see them clearly. You choose to do ABSOLUTELY NOTHING about them. No emotion can control you. Nothing can knock you down. No one can get to you. You are literally invincible.

The good news is it's not an innate skill, so everyone can build it.

The bad news is it's EXTREMELY DIFFICULT to build. The difficulty lies not in actions, but in repetitions.

To build physical strength, you go to the gym, do exercises that challenge your current physical strength, take rests, then repeat.

To build emotional strength, you **expose yourself to difficulties**, take actions that challenge your current emotional strength, **bounce back**, then **repeat**.

It's extremely hard, but if you don't want emotions to fuck you up again and again and again, there's no other way. The few people with true emotional strength built it involuntarily through extreme hardships in life.

Build emotional strength, or the rest don't matter.

&nbsp;

## 2. You create your own reality.
Once you are in the game, you are no longer in the objective reality (i.e., computation). Your subjective reality is an illusion. But that's not what we are discussing here.

You are a player in this game. You want to get something out of it. You define your own objectives and tasks. You define what "winning" means to you. If so, you need to understand:

**You are the winning condition.**

It doesn't matter what you want out of the game. The progression of your story is dependent on you, the main character. If you don't progress, the story cannot progress. If you are not on par with what you want, you cannot get it. Plain and simple.

Think of any multiplayer game you've played. Your satisfaction from it is purely dependent on how good you are at it. Same as this game of life.

```{note} Example
If you want the beachhouse, become someone who can make a shit ton of money.

If you want to change the world, become someone with real power to move it.

If you want a ring in the NBA, become the exceptional player no team can win without.

If you want food, become a good cook.

If you want love, become someone worth loving.

If you want friends, become interesting and socially adept.

If you want opportunities, become so compelling they find you.

If you want creative work that matters, become a relentless craftsman.

If you want peace, become emotionally strong enough to maintain it.
```

Focus on building yourself.

&nbsp;

## 3. You reap what you sow.
If you take an action, you cause an effect.

Depending on the uncontrollable external conditions, the effect can change in **magnitude**, but not in **direction**.

Regardless of the conditions you are in, there's ALWAYS an optimal action which maximizes your benefits or minimizes your losses.

Think about your action space before execution.

&nbsp;

## 4. You are dying.
Time is the single most important resource for everyone and everything.

You CANNOT replenish time. You are ALWAYS spending it involuntarily. You NEVER know how much time you have left.

You are dying. You will die.

Prioritize time management.

&nbsp;

## 5. You own nothing.
**Change & Death** are the only two certainties in reality.

Everything is changing and everything is dying. Everything comes and goes. You own it now, you lose it later. You own it for the rest of your life, you lose it at your death bed.

"At least I own it for my lifetime!" Sure. You have expectations of your "possession", then it changes beyond your expectations, so you adjust your expectations to "fix the ownership", then it changes again, so you adjust again, then it changes again, so you adjust again, ... You've wasted tons of time and energy, then you lose it anyway.

Be prepared to lose everything.
