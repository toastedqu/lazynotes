# Laws
All laws are derived from computation as the absolute reality. Even if you do NOT believe in computation, the following laws ALWAYS hold. I will explain each law without referring to computation.

All laws are sorted in the order of progression.

## 0. Don't take life seriously.
Life experience is an illusion.

Even if you inject meaning into certain aspects of the illusion, they will die anyway. It will die anyway. You will die anyway. Memento Mori.

When you take nothing seriously, you enjoy everything automatically.

&nbsp;

If you embody this law, you can stop here.

If you want some meaning out of this illusion, keep reading.

&nbsp;

## 1. You reap what you sow.
If you take an action, you cause an effect.

Depending on the uncontrollable external conditions, the effect can change in **magnitude**, but not in **direction**.

Hence, under all conditions, there's ALWAYS an optimal action which maximizes your benefits or minimizes your losses.

Think about your action space before execution.

&nbsp;

## 2. You are dying.
Time is the single most important resource for everyone and everything.

You CANNOT replenish time. You are ALWAYS spending it involuntarily. You NEVER know how much time you have left.

You are dying. You will die.

Prioritize time management.

&nbsp;

## 3. You own nothing.
**Change & Death** are the only two certainties in reality.

Everything is changing and everything is dying. Everything comes and goes. You own it now, you lose it later. You own it for the rest of your life, you lose it at your death bed.

"At least I own it for my lifetime!" Sure. You have expectations of your "possession", then it changes beyond your expectations, so you adjust your expectations to "fix the ownership", then it changes again, so you adjust again, then it changes again, so you adjust again, ... You've wasted tons of time and energy, then you lose it anyway.

Be prepared to lose everything.
