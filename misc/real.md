# Reality
The ONLY reality of the observable universe is **computation**.

Karma in Buddhism. Dao in Taoism. Change in I Ching. Fate in Fatalism. God's plan in Christianity. They differ in definitions and properties, but they all share one fundamental building block - computation.

The reason why computation is the sole force behind our reality doesn't matter.

The fact that computation is the sole force behind our reality matters because a deep understanding of this fact can significantly help you live an authentic, fulfilling life, regardless of your current circumstance.

I'm a Zen Buddhist, so I will explain computation from a Zen Buddhist perspective. The most difficult part about this document is the depth of understanding. At the beginning, you may understand what I wrote verbally, then theoretically, then mentally, then emotionally, but a physical understanding ingrained in your brain and your body is EXTREMELY difficult to achieve. Buddhists spend their lifetime trying to achieve that, and most still fail.

## Computation
**Computation is the transition from one (static) state to another**. A state is the set of values of all variables at a specific time point. A variable can be anything, tangible or intangible, real or imaginary, doesn't matter. You will see why later.

**Computation operates at any temporal-spatial scale**. It can be as small as a quark, as large as the whole universe, as short as the Planck time, as long as the entire cosmological decade.

> When inspecting anything, feel free to choose your own scale of computation and states.

Before going deep into computation, kindly understand the following 2 concepts:
- **Phenomenon** (法): Any mental object or factor of existence.
    - e.g., Flower is a phenomenon.
- **Attribute** (相): A perceivable, identifiable, distinguishable feature of a phenomenon.
    - e.g., The color, shape, odor of a flower are attributes.

### Core Properties
Computation has 3 core properties:
- **Impermanence**: The most fundamental property of computation is **change**. Change is dynamic. At any given time point, a state dies, and a new state emerges. Every state is dying, and every new state is emerging. There's no eternally fixed variable in reality.
- **Unity / Emptiness**: Every phenomenon is dependent on each other, either directly or indirectly, doesn't matter. Every phenomenon is dependent on each other because the reality of the observable universe is just a gigantic computation network. No phenomenon can exist on its own, meaning no phenomenon exists in reality in the way we think (with a self identity that is separable from other phenomena).
- **Non-dualism**: All dualistic labels are human concepts created and reinforced by computation throughout evolution due to survival instinct. Computation is, intrinsically, just there. There's no dualistic property for computation by nature. Therefore, there's no dualistic property for anything by nature (including birth and death). No attribute exists in reality.

> Why is everything designed to die?

Too abstract? I love flowers. Let's look at a flower.

- **Impermanence**: A flower never "began" on its own. Sunlight pours energy into a seed. Soil, rain, air, bacteria, and a bee that pollinated some parent plant months ago all converge at one point in spacetime, and we call that convergence "a flower". It is just a node where countless threads of the computation network briefly cross. Then a human cuts it, a florist prices it, packs it, ships it, sells it, and the instant money changes hands the flower stops being mere biology and becomes a symbol: love at a wedding, grief at a funeral, an apology, a birthday. That meaning exists nowhere in the petals. It lives only in human minds, assigned from outside. The flower sits in a vase, the assigned meaning fades as attention drifts elsewhere, the cells lose water, it wilts, decomposes, and returns to the soil, which becomes the condition for the next seed. "Birth" and "death" are just two more labels stamped onto one continuous, dependent process. Nothing was ever truly created or destroyed. The network only rearranged itself.
- **Unity / Emptiness**: Take the flower apart and ask "where is the flower?" Pull off the petals, the stamen, the pistil, the sepals, the stem, the leaves. Point to any one piece: is that the flower? No. So keep dissecting. Each petal is just cells. Each cell is organelles, water, and proteins. Each protein is a chain of molecules. Each molecule is atoms. Each atom is a nucleus and electrons, then quarks and fields and probability. At no layer do you ever find a hidden "flower-essence" sitting inside. The "flower" was only ever a label we painted over a temporary arrangement of parts that are themselves arrangements of parts - labels all the way down, with no self-standing object at the bottom.
- **Non-dualism**: We slice the world of flowers into neat dualities: a red rose means passion, a white lily means death, a chrysanthemum is celebratory in one culture and funereal in another, a "weed" is just a flower someone decided was unwanted. Beautiful versus ugly, lucky versus unlucky, sacred versus ordinary - none of these pairs is found anywhere in the flower itself. Scan the cells, the pigments, the wavelengths of reflected light: you will find chemistry, but not "passion", not "mourning", not "good" or "bad". The opposites live entirely on our side of the network, projected outward and then mistaken for properties of the thing. And every label only has meaning by leaning on its opposite - "beautiful" is empty without "ugly", "sacred" needs "profane" to mean anything, so the dualities don't even stand on their own - they arise together, define each other, and dissolve together. Strip the projections away and there is no red-rose-passion and no white-lily-grief, no two opposing things at all - just one undivided process the network is running, which we then carve into pairs purely for our own convenience.

> Similarly, think about a human being.

In short, **the reality is a (dynamic) process**. No static state represents the true reality. No object exists with a separable self identity in reality. No attribute of any object exists in reality, because, the reality is a process.

### Human-specific properties
Some preliminary Buddhist concepts about humans:
- **Five Aggregates** (五蕴): Form, Feeling, Perception, Mental Formation, Consciousness.
- **Six Sense Bases** (六识): Eye, Ear, Nose, Tongue, Body, Mind.

Computation absolutely doesn't give a fuck about humans. Here's why:
- **Uncertain**: Uncertainty is the scariest concept to most humans because it directly threatens survival. Why? No phenomenon has a separable self identity, so a phenomenon is almost always dependent on far more phenomena than anyone could possibly imagine. Theoretically, at any time point, we can ALWAYS predict the next state from the current state as long as we have ALL the information. Practically, it is often impossible for anyone to obtain all the information precisely because all phenomena are dependent on each other. The lack of such information leads to prediction errors, which quantifies the uncertainty we often ignore in various circumstances. Therefore, from a human's perspective, **nothing is deterministic**.
- **Uncontrollable**: So many religions, philosophical ideologies, and psychological theories are telling humans to stop trying to control things, yet most don't listen by heart. Why? Because control gives a sense of safety, which is crucial for survival. Unfortunately, all phenomena depend on each other, so the direct influence of a phenomenon to another is, in most cases, quite negligible. For real. We don't even have total control over our own Five Aggregates. More importantly, we think we "influence" things, but it's actually just computation doing its own thing. There's no "we", and there's no "things". **Control is an illusion**, which leads to the next point.
- **Illusory**: This is the last but the most mind-fucking horror story of computation. Every single state is computed by computation. Every single pheonomena at any given time point is computed by computation. Every single attribute at any given time point is computed by computation. Therefore, the current state of our Five Aggregates and Six Sense Bases is computed by computation. Therefore, **our entire life experience is nothing but a movie directed by computation**. We are not even the actors because there's no actor. An "actor" phenomenon is also computed by computation. When an angry Redditor shouts "Everything is BS!", they are right. Every event in our life experience is essentially bullshit computed by computation.

> "Free will" is computed by computation. Change my mind.

What's even more mind-fucking is, you might think this is horrible and overly pessimistic. No. An in-depth understanding of the nature of computation is actually the best blessing you could get in life.

## Life
For most of us, life is good when there's no problem, and life is bad when there is a problem. Essentially, our mental well-being is conditioned on life events.

It doesn't need to be. The conditionality is ingrained in our genes because *Survival instinct is the origin of life experience*.

To drop this conditionality, you have to see reality - Your life experience is a movie directed (and acted) by computation. Your life is a process of continuous state transitions. There is no "thing". There is only process.

### Problem
*Attachment is the root of suffering.* True, and I'd like to further expand it:

> *Suffering is the rejection of reality.*

Suffering is NOT pain. Camus' Sisyphus did NOT suffer despite being doomed to push a boulder up the hill forever. If you can tolerate pain when pain is your reality, you don't suffer. Conversely, if you cannot accept joy when joy is your reality, you suffer.

Specifically, suffering takes place in 3 forms according to Buddhism, where the last includes the first 2:
- **Greed** (贪): Cling to what you want.
- **Hatred** (嗔): Push away what you don't want.
- **Ignorance** (痴): Ignore reality.

If you cannot get what you want, if you lose what you love, if you keep getting what you don't want, if you cannot lose what you hate, you suffer. 

Your life experience is conditioned on your "ownership" of those "things" because you interpret them as "good" or "bad". This conditionality assumes control, certainty, staticity, dualism, and existence of "self". All of these assumptions are illusions created and reinforced by computation, originating from survival instinct. 

This conditionality itself is ignorance. We actively reject reality when facing difficult situations because **we often don't see reality**. As soon as we are born, our movies start, and we think we are the main characters in these movies, and we aim to optimize our survival capabilities at any given time point because of survival instinct. Computation is doing its work, and we get so absorbed into our own movies that we no longer see reality.

Suppose in a snippet of your movie, you applied to your dream company. You nailed every round till the final interview, and the interviewer just didn't like you for whatever unknown reason, and you got rejected. Months of preparations became obsolete. Now, what do you do? If you don't see reality, you'd probably get angry or sad or cry or curse the interviewer or facetime your family and friends to rant or whatever, or if your emotional regulation skill is strong you'd move on quickly. Doesn't matter. At this stage, you still believe you are the actor in the movie.

Let's rewind the human-specific properties of computation (i.e., reality). 
- **Illusory**: If you see reality, you already understand you are in an illusory movie directed (and acted) by computation. Getting rejected by your dream company is merely a computation result.
- **Uncontrollable**: The computation result is beyond your control because you literally have no control in reality. It is not even in the control of the final interviewer. They don't even know why they didn't like you. Computation made them dislike you at that specific time point for whatever reason, probably genetics and negative life experiences. Doesn't matter.
- **Uncertain**: You gave your best shot. You put your best possible effort to give the best possible interview performance, but personal preferences are factors that are unpredictable. The interview outcome is unpredictable from the beginning. You simply maximized your probability of getting an offer, but you can never alter reality toward a favorable outcome for you.

Now, let's rewind the core properties of computation (i.e., reality).
- **Non-dualism**: The rejection just happened. That's it. You attached a negative label to it because computation told you so. How? Computation shaped your Five Aggregates through various life events to make you perceive this company as the best possible match for you, and you perceive "not getting what you want" as negative because your survival capability is no longer optimized, all according to computation. You can also perceive the same event as positive by saying "Wow, I've learnt an incredible life lesson!" but computation didn't choose this path. You feel negative because of computation, when in reality, this event is neutral by nature. 
    - When you feel negative, the occurrence of the feeling has become part of reality as well. Therefore, you feeling negative is also neutral by nature. This reaction to a life event is called **"second arrow"** in Buddhism, and you can easily shoot more arrows on top of this, like "Why am I feeling negative again? I should feel positive/neutral!" Then you enter an endless spiral of thoughts and emotions.
    - When dissected this way, you might find it funny and easily manageable. However, this happens way more often than people think. We will address the solution later.
- **Unity / Emptiness**: If you curse the interviewer, you'd understand where they come from - family stress, job stress, demographic bias, bad mood, or they really just hate you personally, whatever. They didn't choose this. Computation did. You happen to be affected by this computation result. That's it.
    - Compassion & Forgiveness is a critical topic in Buddhism, but I won't force it. You don't need to forgive them for ruining your life. Understanding reality alone would alleviate your suffering.
- **Impermanence**: This too shall pass. In fact, this event has already passed. You holding the event in your mind is the reason why you think this has not passed yet.

So what is the reality in this specific case? "You got rejected by the final interviewer." Period. Nothing more. Nothing less. If you add anything to it - interpretation, analysis, ration, emotion, or whatever - the occurrence of these things is real, but the content of these things is illusory.

### Solution

> Just see reality.

Now you know what reality is - Just computation. No "self-identity" in anything. No staticity. No permanence. No dualism - What's left for you to do is to just see it. As soon as you see reality, you are at peace.

"Peace" here is a bit different from what you may think. It does NOT mean the pain or negativity you feel will just disappear. No. They are here. You feel them. This is still part of reality. However, you see this now. The seeing is also part of reality. "Peace" here feels more like "The pain is here. The negativitiy is here. I am here. Ok."

Obviously much easier said than done, or everyone would have been at peace already.

The difficulty lies in the seeing - How often can you see it? How long can you see it for? How easy is it for you to see it in different scenarios?
